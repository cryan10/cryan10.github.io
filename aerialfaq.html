What is the difference between Aerial Dance and Aerial Yoga?

Aerial yoga is great foundation for aerial dance. It focuses on conditioning, strength building, flexibility, inverting, and balance, all which are very important to aerial dance. Aerial Dance builds on these basic aerial yoga moves and uses them for aerial dance sequences turning you into an aerial ballerina!


What is Aerial Hoop (Lyra)?

An aerial hoop (also known as lyra or cerceau) is a metal apparatus suspended in the air used for dancing and performing aerial acrobatics.


What are the benefits of Aerial classes?

It will build muscle, tone your body, increase flexibility, increase balance, increase self confidence, and also gives you bragging rights!


What should I wear to class?

Wear something you are comfortable in. I recommend nothing super loose because it can get caught in the fabric. Also we do invert, keep that in mind when picking out your outfit! Absolutely no jewelry! this can cause rips and tears in the fabric. When taking a lyra class we recommend you also were socks to protect your feet.

Is there a weight limit?

The weight limit on our equipment is 396 lbs.


Do I need prior experience?

No, we will teach you the basics and help you build your strength along the way!


How many students are in class?

We allow 4 students in our Lyra class and 6 in any of our Aerial Yoga/Dance classes. We currently have 2 Lyras and 6 hammocks. This allows us to have small classes sizes and focus on the skill level of the individual.
