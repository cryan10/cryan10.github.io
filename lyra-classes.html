Lyra / Aerial Hoop


Schedule a Class

AERIAL HOOP, also known as Lyra, is an exciting, fun challenge to add to your workout routine. Lyra is a metal hoop suspended from the ceiling that is used to perform various acrobatic and circus-like tricks. We offer beginner and intermediate level classes that will help build strength and increase flexibility. This class is perfect cross training for pole or aerial silks students, but no experience is necessary to attend your first class. Each student progresses at a different level and classes are individually tailored to each student.

Class Descriptions


Beginners Lyra

This is a beginner level class that teaches low flow lyra moves to help students gain better body awareness and control. In this class, students work on basic choreography and spins. No prior pole or aerial experience is needed.


Lyra (All Levels)

Lyra helps you strengthen and tone while creating better body awareness and control. This class is a beginner to intermediate level class. All students learn basic poses and transitions and progress into more intermediate level tricks as they become more experienced. Students advance at their own pace and the instructor modifies moves to fit each student's level.


Open Practice

Open studio time to come and work on your tricks on your own time. Someone will be around to monitor, but there will be no instruction at this time. This is a time to work on the moves you have already learned during class time. Must be a member of Be Studios for open practice. If you are not a member or are from out of town please call us for approval to attend open practice. Open practice rate is free for members and $10 per hour for non-members if approved.

Rachael

Rachael’s aerial style is dramatic. She loves to dance to darker song lyrics to show there is grace through life’s toughest times. She started her aerial journey with pole dance because she wanted to exercise in a way that created art. For a long time she struggled with severe depression and with the help of pole she was taken off of medicine and found joy in every aspect of life. Rachael’s favorite thing about aerial arts is the expression of love for one's self. She learned to love herself through depression because of pole. She wants to share that love with her students and hopes that her students feel encouraged in class. She wants them to know that they are capable of amazing things they did not know they could do. Rachael has been involved in aerial arts since 2015 and started teaching in the spring of 2017.

Outside of Be Fitness, Rachael enjoys knitting, adventuring to new places, trying new foods and working to make others' lives more fulfilling. She also loves to design clothing and studied fashion at the University of Akron.

Emily

Emily loves all aspects of aerial dance, but when it comes to choreography she prefers to keep it slightly more aggressive. She likes fast paced, high silk climbing with dramatic drops. She loves aerial silks because of the endless possibilities that they provide to express yourself. You can be great at floor work and incorporate a lot of it in your routines. Or you can be awful at floor work and focus more on aerial tricks. Your dance style can be graceful or aggressive, or a mix of both. The creativity is endless!

Emily started doing aerial silks as a fun, easy way to work out because she hated to go to the gym. She had no idea how much it would become a part of her life! She has been doing aerial silks since 2012 and teaching since 2105. She hopes that her students gain the tools they need to find their own style of dance, while also pushing their limits to conquer routines and transitions that they may not master right away.

Outside of aerial silks, Emily loves her pets and rescuing fur babies. She likes to ride her motorcycle and she also has three kids who take up most of her time. They spend their weekends camping, fishing, and boating. She has a bachelor’s degree in Biology from Cleveland State University and really just loves anything science related.
