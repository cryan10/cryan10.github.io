KIDS CLASSES are offered for aerial hammock and ballet barre. These classes offer your child the opportunity to explore fitness in new, exciting ways. Class Descriptions


Kids Aerial Dance

This class is for kids ages 6-13 and focuses on basic poses and transitions using the aerial hammock. Students learn balance, coordination, body awareness, flexibility, and strength.


Kids Ballet Barre

This class is for kids ages 6-13 and focuses on grace, flexibility, and control using traditional ballet barre techniques.

Kids Aerial Workshops

Our 8-week long workshop focuses on creating a solo aerial routine to be performed at the end of the series. Each student will get to choose between creating a routine on the aerial hammock or the lyra. Students spend the first week learning and choosing their tricks and picking out a song. Our instructor choreographs the routine based on these moves and begins to teach the routine during the second week. Students then spend the following weeks learning and perfecting the choreography so that they are ready for their big performance!

Tori Muellauer


Tori has been dancing for 22 years and is thrilled to be working with Be Studios. She has trained in Ballet, Jazz, Tap, Modern, Contemporary, Hip Hop, and Musical Theater. Tori has a BA degree in Dance from Slippery Rock University and is currently teaching and performing all over the Cleveland area. Tori started her journey at Be Studios in 2015 and has loved combining her classical dance training with the amazing and unique art forms Be Studios has to offer.

One of the things Tori loves most about dance is it allows a freedom of expression through movement when words aren't quite right. She wants her students to feel like they can express themselves and move through a sense of poise and grace while still building technical skills that can be used in all dance forms.

Emily Griffith


Emily loves all aspects of aerial dance, but when it comes to choreography she prefers to keep it slightly more aggressive. She likes fast paced, high silk climbing with dramatic drops. She loves aerial silks because of the endless possibilities that they provide to express yourself. You can be great at floor work and incorporate a lot of it in your routines. Or you can be awful at floor work and focus more on aerial tricks. Your dance style can be graceful or aggressive, or a mix of both. The creativity is endless!

Emily started doing aerial silks as a fun, easy way to work out because she hated to go to the gym. She had no idea how much it would become a part of her life! She has been doing aerial silks since 2012 and teaching since 2105. She hopes that her students gain the tools they need to find their own style of dance, while also pushing their limits to conquer routines and transitions that they may not master right away.

Outside of aerial arts, Emily loves her pets and rescuing fur babies. She likes to ride her motorcycle and she also has three kids who take up most of her time.
