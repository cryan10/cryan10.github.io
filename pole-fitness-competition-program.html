Pole Fitness Competition Program

BE STUDIOS' POLE FITNESS COMPETITION PROGRAM guides students on their journey to compete in regional pole fitness competitions across the US. The program offers workshops, team practices and emotional support to help students prepare for competition. Our program encourages men and women to step out of their comfort zones to find courage and strength to participate in a life-changing performance.




Our Workshops

Our competition program consists of __ workshops throughout the course of 14 weeks. Each workshop is focused around the Pole Sport Organization's (PSO) scoring elements. For more information on PSO, please visit www.PoleSportOrg.com .

Brittany Hansel

Brittany doesn’t have a specific pole style but tends to gravitate towards strength moves. Dancing “sexy” doesn’t come naturally but she still tries! She hopes that her students find a safe place to express themselves and celebrate their accomplishments in her classes. She believes that the best part about teaching is how each student is different and brings their own style to class. She’s emphasizes correct form and provides lots of spotting during class to help prevent injuries.

Brittany loves that she can make pole whatever she wants. She can dance happy, mad, sad, and can use pole for a workout or for mental clarity. Ultimately, the pole family/sisterhood is what keeps her coming back. She loves unique transitions and combos and doesn’t have a specific favorite trick. She’s been pole dancing since 2012 and started teaching in 2015. She got into pole because of a YouTube video she watched of Jenyne Butterfly when she was younger and always wanted to do it since then. She started during graduate school at BW and it was the best release for all her stress.

Outside of pole, Brittany grew up racing BMX and still rides her bike a lot. She has a Golden Retriever fur-baby (Val) who loves to swim in the lake. She’s a hyperactive person so she’s always doing something like baking or crafting when she has time off.

Tori Muellauer

Tori has been dancing for 22 years and is thrilled to be working with Be Studios. She has trained in Ballet, Jazz, Tap, Modern, Contemporary, Hip Hop, and Musical Theater. Tori has a BA degree in Dance from Slippery Rock University and is currently teaching and performing all over the Cleveland area. Tori started her pole journey at Be Studios in 2015 and has loved combining her classical dance training with this amazing and unique art form.

One of the things Tori loves most about dance and pole is that they allow a freedom of expression through movement when words aren't quite right. She wants her students to feel like they can express themselves and move through a sense of poise and grace while still building technical skills that can be used in all dance forms.

Carrie Marie

Carrie's pole style places emphasis on movement and dance technique. For her, pole dance is not a sport; it is an art form. She believes that pointy toes, pretty lines, lyricism and musicality are more important than learning the latest, trendy pole trick. She encourages her students to utilize pole dance as a form of self-expression and creative outlet, not just as a workout.

Carrie has been pole dancing since 2014. Prior to her pole journey, she was a competitive Irish dancer for 12 years. She continues to grow as a dancer by taking hip hop and stylized heels classes. Her favorite pole tricks are spin combos and flexibility tricks.

Outside of pole, Carrie is a classical guitarist and singer-songwriter. In her muggle life, she is a web developer and created this website.


Maya Soul

After taking her first class at Be Studios, Maya Soul fell in love. The studio became her home, and pole dance allowed her to unfold. "I've overcome fear and shame," she says, "and released control to allow life to flow."

Maya started teaching workshops and classes in 2013, and became a competitive pole athlete in 2017. Her style is diverse, which is one of the reasons she loves pole. "Pole doesn’t have to be defined," she says. "You have complete freedom of expression. One moment you can be exotic, dramatic the next, powerful, vulnerable, sexy, sad, even angry."

In class, Maya empowers her students to believe in themselves and build lasting confidence that transcends the studio. She attests that Be Studios is a family and believes that if she shares her passion to light your fire, then her job is done.

Outside of pole, Maya enjoys psychology, business and good company.
