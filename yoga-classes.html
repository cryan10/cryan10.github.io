Class Descriptions


Yoga & Tea

This is a Vinyasa Flow Yoga class followed by a holistic tea offering. Each week the class centers around a different tea with movements that will allow the body to reap the tea's benefits.

The practice starts slow and gentle to warm up the body and prepare it for an energizing practice. Students work up to a faster pace to create fire within and move energy throughout the body. The class slowly progresses into inversions with modifications for every level. Students leave class feeling refreshed, rejuvenated, and restored. The class's purpose is to create an empowering and cleansing practice focused on letting go and creating space within our mind and bodies.

Holistic tea formulas are offered after savasana to help you to ease back into your day. We have locally prepared tea formulas from Harris Holistic Wellness, all certified organic and fair trade, and ethically sourced from around the world.

For more information about Harris Holistic Wellness, please visit their Facebook page .


Power Vinyasa

Power Vinyasa Flow is an invigorating Vinyasa Flow with extended holds and slow, controlled movements sprinkled into the practice to help students build strength and calm the mind. All levels of experience welcome!


Restorative Aerial Yoga

This is an all-levels class where students use aerial hammocks to gently support restorative yoga poses and mindful stretches. The class ends with a long relaxation stage in the hammock cocoon. Although the class is gentle and slow-paced, students gain flexibility and mindfulness from tihs class.


Intro to Aerial Yoga

This is a beginner level class which uses the silks in a low level position to help introduce the student to the fabric and aerial yoga. It offers easy use for students to help gain confidence lower to the ground before moving up to a higher level fabric, but still pushes the body towards a better flexibility and a more open mind.


Aerial Yoga

This is an all-levels class where students use aerial hammocks to gently support aerial poses and mindful stretches. Aerial yoga is great foundation for aerial dance. You will learn many of the aerial postures while focusing on conditioning, strength building, flexibility, inverting, and balance, all which are very important to aerial dance. The class ends with a long relaxation stage in the hammock cocoon.

Wanida Joy

Wanida teaches Pilates, Yoga, and Spinning at Be Fitness. She is also available for private pole lessons and will occasionally sub pole classes. She started teaching pole at Be in 2015 after falling in love with the freedom and expression in dancing. She has been practicing yoga for about 10 years and completed her RYT-200 training in 2015. She is also certified in aerial yoga. She completed her Pilates Reformer Level 1 and Spin training in 2019 and plans to complete her Level 2 Pilates training in 2020.

Wanida believes that movement heals the body and mind, whether you are dancing, practicing yoga or Pilates, or doing acrobatic tricks in the air. She hopes that her students find a sense of peace and appreciation for their bodies and movements throughout class. She believes that the most important part of class is learning and loving what your body is capable of doing. She hopes to help students feel empowered and confident in their own body and mind by creating better body awareness and control. She aims to give students a safe place to explore and be themselves with no judgement so that they can lead a more authentic life both in and out of the studio.

Outside of Be, Wanida lives with her boyfriend Joe, her dog Oliver, and their cat Gamora. She is learning to play golf and has noticed a significant improvement in her game since she started practicing Pilates on a more regular basis. She also collects sneakers and loves everything Sanrio, Marvel, and Tokidoki.

Rachel

Rachel loves Cleveland and she loves yoga, what more is there to tell? She found yoga at a difficult point in her life, when everything had been shaken up due to a sudden and unexpected medical issue. Yoga profoundly changed Rachel's life. It helped her find her inner strength, taught her patience, self-love and the beauty of enjoying the process. Years later, she still can't get enough of it! Rachel's classes are challenging, empowering and supportive. She strives to help her students be their best self, on and off the mat.

Rachel completed her 200 hour CYT, RYT teacher training in 2015 at Cleveland Yoga. Since then, she has completed additional trainings with internationally recognized teacher Kathryn Budig, and extensive anatomy immersions with Gina Schatz.

Emily

Emily loves all aspects of aerial dance but, when it comes to choreography, she prefers to keep it slightly more aggressive. She likes fast paced, high silk climbing with dramatic drops. She loves aerial silks because of the endless possibilities that they provide to express yourself. You can be great at floor work and incorporate a lot of it in your routines. Or you can be awful at floor work and focus more on aerial tricks. Your dance style can be graceful or aggressive, or a mix of both. The creativity is endless!

Emily started doing aerial silks as a fun, easy way to work out because she hated going to the gym. She had no idea how much it would become a part of her life! She has been doing aerial silks since 2012 and teaching since 2015. She hopes that her students gain the tools they need to find their own style of dance, while also pushing their limits to conquer routines and transitions that they may not master right away.

Outside of aerial silks, Emily loves her pets and rescuing fur babies. She likes to ride her motorcycle and she also has three kids who take up most of her time. They spend their weekends camping, fishing, and boating. She has a bachelor’s degree in Biology from Cleveland State University and really just loves anything science related.

Brittney

Brittney first started her yoga journey in 2011 because she struggled with high anxiety and chronic stress. Yoga helped change her life for the better; she grew both physically and mentally because of the practice. She learned so much about herself through mindfulness and inner peace.

In today's hectic world, Brittney wants to offer her students a chance to calm their minds and relax their bodies through the art of aerial yoga. Her goal is to guide her students through a series of yoga poses and stretches that increase flexibility, balance and strength. She create judgment-free classes where people with all levels of experience can feel safe and comfortable to practice yoga.
