Aerial Dance

at our Cleveland location


Schedule a Class

AERIAL DANCE is the perfect addition to your fitness routine. We offer classes in aerial silks and aerial hammock, ribbons that are suspended from the ceiling as either one or two pieces, to perform various circus and aerial acrobatic tricks. Aerial silks utilizes full body strength and control to safely execute different climbs, drops, and wraps. This class is perfect cross-training for pole or aerial hoop students, but no experience is necessary to attend your first class. Each student progresses at a different level and classes are individually tailored to each student.

Class Descriptions


Beginners Aerial Dance

Have you ever been interested in learning aerial dance? This class will help tone, strengthen and increase flexibility while teaching you seamless sequences to begin your practices in the aerial arts! Students learn basic technique and poses in the aerial hammock.


Aerial Dance (All Levels)

This class will help you tone, strengthen and increase your flexibility as you learn seamless sequences to further your practice in the aerial arts! All students learn poses and transitions with the aerial hammock. With consistent practice, intermediate students have the option to learn open aerial silks. This class is a beginner to intermediate level class. Students advance at their own pace and the instructor modifies moves to fit each student's level.


Restorative Aerial Yoga

This is an all-levels class where students use aerial hammocks to gently support restorative yoga poses and mindful stretches. The class ends with a long relaxation stage in the hammock cocoon. Although the class is gentle and slow-paced, students gain flexibility and mindfulness from tihs class.

Rachael Horton


Rachael’s aerial style is dramatic. She loves to dance to darker song lyrics to show there is grace through life’s toughest times. She started her aerial journey with pole dance because she wanted to exercise in a way that created art. For a long time she struggled with severe depression and with the help of pole she was taken off of medicine and found joy in every aspect of life. Rachael’s favorite thing about aerial arts is the expression of love for one's self. She learned to love herself through depression because of pole. She wants to share that love with her students and hopes that her students feel encouraged in class. She wants them to know that they are capable of amazing things they did not know they could do. Rachael has been involved in aerial arts since 2015 and started teaching in the spring of 2017.

Outside of Be Studios, Rachael enjoys knitting, adventuring to new places, trying new foods and working to make others' lives more fulfilling. She also loves to design clothing and studied fashion at the University of Akron.

Emily Griffith


Emily loves all aspects of aerial dance but, when it comes to choreography, she prefers to keep it slightly more aggressive. She likes fast paced, high silk climbing with dramatic drops. She loves aerial silks because of the endless possibilities that they provide to express yourself. You can be great at floor work and incorporate a lot of it in your routines. Or you can be awful at floor work and focus more on aerial tricks. Your dance style can be graceful or aggressive, or a mix of both. The creativity is endless!

Emily started doing aerial silks as a fun, easy way to work out because she hated going to the gym. She had no idea how much it would become a part of her life! She has been doing aerial silks since 2012 and teaching since 2015. She hopes that her students gain the tools they need to find their own style of dance, while also pushing their limits to conquer routines and transitions that they may not master right away.

Outside of aerial silks, Emily loves her pets and rescuing fur babies. She likes to ride her motorcycle and she also has three kids who take up most of her time. They spend their weekends camping, fishing, and boating. She has a bachelor’s degree in Biology from Cleveland State University and really just loves anything science related.

Brittney Bloniak


Brittney first started her yoga journey in 2011 because she struggled with high anxiety and chronic stress. Yoga helped change her life for the better; she grew both physically and mentally because of the practice. She learned so much about herself through mindfulness and inner peace.

In today's hectic world, Brittney wants to offer her students a chance to calm their minds and relax their bodies through the art of aerial yoga. Her goal is to guide her students through a series of yoga poses and stretches that increase flexibility, balance and strength. She create judgment-free classes where people with all levels of experience can feel safe and comfortable to practice yoga.
