Class Descriptions


Restorative Aerial Yoga

This is an all-levels class which uses the silks in a low level position to gently support restorative poses as well as mindful stretches and a long relaxation stage at the end of class in the hammock cocoon. It offers easy use for students with physical limitations but still pushes the body towards a better flexibility and a more open mind.


Yoga & Tea

This class is a Vinyasa Flow Yoga class followed by a Holistic Tea Offering. Each week the class centers around a different tea with movements that will allow the body to reap the tea's benefits.

The practice will start slow and gentle to warm up the body and prepare it for an energizing practice. We will work up to a faster pace creating fire within and moving energy throughout the body. This is an all-levels class that will slowly progress into inversions with modifications for every level. You will leave class feeling refreshed, rejuvenated, and restored. Our purpose is to create an empowering and cleansing practice focused on letting go and creating space within our mind and bodies.

We will be offering holistic tea formulas after savasana allowing you to ease back into your day. We have locally prepared tea formulas from Harris Holistic Wellness, all certified organic and fair trade, and ethically sourced from around the world.

Emily Griffith


Emily loves all aspects of aerial dance, but when it comes to choreography she prefers to keep it slightly more aggressive. She likes fast pace, high silk climbing with dramatic drops. She loves aerial silks because of the endless possibilities that they provide to express yourself. You can be great at floor work and incorporate a lot of your routine as that. Or you can be awful at floor work and focus more on in the air moves. Your dance can reflect ballet style routine or a more aggressive routine. The creative they allow is endless!

Emily started doing aerial silks as a fun easy way to work out because she hated to go to the gym. She had no idea how much of it would become a part of her life! She has been doing aerial silks for 5 years and teaching for almost 2 years. She hopes that her students gain the tools they need to open up their creativity and find their own style of dance, while also pushing their limits and helping them conquer routines and transitions that they may not master right away. As for herself, she prefers does that are strengthening. Anything that she can’t get not he first try and actually require her to work towards.

Outside of aerial silks, she loves her pets and rescuing fur babies. She likes to ride her motorcycle and she also has three kids who take up most of her time. They spend their weekends camping, fishing, and boating. She has a bachelor’s degree in Biology from Cleveland State and really just loves anything science related.

Wanida Joy


Wanida first tried yoga and meditation as a teenager under the suggestion of her parents. She struggled with it as a teen and it wasn’t until she was in her 20s that she was able to have a regular practice. She began her journey looking for a way to cope with anxiety and depression and now has been practicing for nearly 10 years. She strives to make her classes feel safe and comfortable to allow students to let go and relax into the moment and into their bodies. She believes that there is nothing more empowering than to really be in and understand your own body. At the same time she understands how difficult it can be to be still and quiet. After all, it did take her nearly a decade just to get through one hour of yoga. Her classes are more gentle and fluid inspired by movement and dance with a focus on breath and meditation. She hopes that her students leave class feeling relaxed and with a greater sense of love and compassion for themselves.

Wanida received her 200 hour certification in 2015 with Ananda Bhakti. Upon graduation, she took a break to really find her voice and her message as a yoga teacher. She began teaching in the spring of 2016 and taught adult classes for a little over a year before taking a break to focus more on teaching her pole classes and managing Be Studios. Wanida also teaches with Zenworks teaching children in the Cleveland Metropolitan School District. She is very passionate about teaching yoga to help heal trauma and to teach people of all ages to learn to treat themselves with love and compassion.
